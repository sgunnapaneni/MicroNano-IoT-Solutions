Introduction

The rise of Internet of Things (IoT) devices and the growing need for real-time data processing have introduced new challenges in distributed systems. These challenges include:

  • Ensuring scalability
  • Enhancing security
  • Managing resources efficiently

Architectural Solutions

Microservices and nanoservices have emerged as powerful architectural solutions to meet these challenges, enabling modularity, flexibility, and dynamic workload handling in IoT, edge, and cloud computing environments. This website presents a comprehensive survey on these advancements, offering insights into the latest research and trends in microservices and nanoservices.


Performance and Resource Management

This survey delves into the advancements and applications of microservice and nanoservice architectures, presenting a comprehensive analysis of their impact on:

  • Performance
  • Resource management
  • Security

Microservices in Edge Computing

One of the main topics covered is the use of microservices in edge computing and the Internet of Things, where they are essential for data processing, sensor management, and device connection. Nanoservices further lower service granularity and are particularly helpful in settings with limited resources. The ability of deployment strategies to maximize performance in cloud, fog, and edge environments—such as models based on reinforcement learning and graph-based optimization techniques—is also thoroughly investigated.


Security Considerations

In distributed systems, especially in the Internet of Things, security considerations are crucial. The survey examines a number of methods that provide innovative ways to defend microservices and nanoservices from cyberattacks, such as:

  • Federated learning
  • Blockchain-based security frameworks
  • API gateway security

In industries like healthcare, where secure management of sensitive data and real-time monitoring are essential, these solutions are vital for data protection.


Breakthroughs in Healthcare Applications

Microservices and nanoservices are propelling major breakthroughs in IoT and healthcare applications, facilitating:

  • Real-time health monitoring
  • Improved healthcare decision-making
  • Enhanced data analysis from medical sensors

Emerging Trends

A number of emerging trends are also identified by the survey, including:

  • The integration of blockchain technology with IoT, which improves security and transparency in data management
  • AI-driven security awareness, which uses artificial intelligence to anticipate and stop security threats in real-time
  • Federated learning, allowing decentralized data processing while maintaining anonymity

Ongoing Challenges

Notwithstanding these developments, several difficulties still exist, including:

  • Scalability in various contexts
  • Balancing privacy and security issues
  • Optimizing energy use
  • Coordinating communication between microservices and nanoservices

These persistent challenges underscore the need for further research and innovation to fully leverage the potential of these architectures. With insights into both recent developments and potential future research avenues, this survey offers a comprehensive assessment of the state of microservices and nanoservices in cloud, edge, and IoT computing.

  1. Group 1: Architectures for Microservices and Nanoservices

    • Alanezi & Mishra: Utilizing Microservices Architecture for Enhanced Service Sharing in IoT Edge Environments (Paper 1)
    • Zaki et al.: Cloud-Assisted Microservice-Based Framework for Healthcare Systems
    • Pallewatta et al.: Placement of Microservices-based IoT Applications in Fog Computing: A Taxonomy and Future Directions (Paper 10)

    Comprehension of lightweight service-oriented architectures in distributed systems requires a comprehension of the fundamental ideas of microservices and nanoservices, which are examined in this category. The chosen papers explore architectural frameworks that improve deployment and service sharing in IoT edge settings. For example, Alanezi and Mishra's work on the use of microservices architecture emphasizes how microservices can improve service delivery in situations with limited resources, highlighting the synergy between edge computing and the Internet of Things.

    The study by Zaki et al. also presents a cloud-assisted microservice-based framework for healthcare systems, demonstrating real-world microservices implementations in crucial industries. Pallewatta et al. provide a structured method for comprehending the placement difficulties in distributed environments by contributing a taxonomy for microservice deployment in fog computing. When taken as a whole, these papers offer a thorough understanding of the architecture, functionality, and importance of microservices and nanoservices in contemporary Internet of Things applications.


  2. Group 2: Edge and Cloud Deployment and Placement Strategies

    • Pallewatta et al.: Placement of Microservices-based IoT Applications in Fog Computing: A Taxonomy and Future Directions (Paper 10)
    • Taleb et al.: Energy- and Resource-Aware Graph-Based Microservices Placement in the Cloud-Fog-Edge Continuum (Paper 21)
    • Islam et al.: Resource-Aware Dynamic Service Deployment for Local IoT Edge Computing: Healthcare Use Case (Paper 15)

    In order to guarantee effective resource usage and performance optimization, our group focuses on microservices and nanoservices deployment methodologies across edge and cloud platforms. Several deployment models and placement algorithms that provide efficient distributed service management are highlighted in the papers selected for this category. Pallewatta et al.'s study, for instance, sheds light on the taxonomy and difficulties of microservice placement in fog computing, which is essential for maximizing service delivery in edge computing scenarios.

    Additionally, Taleb et al. address important issues in resource allocation throughout the cloud-fog-edge continuum using a unique graph-based method for placing microservices that are resource- and energy-aware. The dynamic service deployment study by Islam et al. demonstrates tactics designed specifically for healthcare applications, guaranteeing prompt service availability in emergency situations. When taken as a whole, these studies highlight the complexity of implementing microservices in a variety of settings and the necessity of flexible approaches that take into account the unique features of cloud and edge computing.


  3. Group 3: Cloud, Edge, and IoT Resource Management and Optimization

    • Harjula et al.: Edge Computing for Industrial IoT: Challenges and Solutions (Paper 9)
    • Gkonis et al.: A Survey on IoT-Edge-Cloud Continuum Systems: Status, Challenges, Use Cases, and Open Issues (Paper 18)
    • Sánchez-Gallegos et al.: On the Continuous Processing of Health Data in Edge-Fog-Cloud Computing by Using Micro/Nanoservice Composition (Paper 16)

    In order to improve performance and scalability, papers in this category concentrate on optimizing task allocation and resource allocation in microservice and nanoservice architectures. The chosen papers look into a range of optimization strategies and algorithms that raise service-oriented architectures' effectiveness in Internet of Things applications. The problems with industrial IoT are discussed by Harjula et al., who offer solutions that use edge computing to minimize latency and maximize resource management.

    A survey on reinforcement learning-based deployment tactics is also presented in the study by Gkonis et al., which highlights creative approaches to dynamic resource allocation in Internet of Things systems. These strategies are essential for guaranteeing that services are effectively administered and provided, especially in settings with fluctuating workloads and limited resources. When taken as a whole, these studies offer a strong foundation for comprehending how optimization strategies might improve microservice and nanoservice architecture performance in practical applications.


  4. Group 4: Service-Oriented Architectures' Scalability and Performance

    • Namasudra et al.: Blockchain-Based Medical Certificate Generation and Verification for IoT-Based Healthcare Systems (Paper 11)
    • Gkonis et al.: A Survey on IoT-Edge-Cloud Continuum Systems: Status, Challenges, Use Cases, and Open Issues (Paper 18)
    • Wen et al.: StatuScale: Status-aware and Elastic Scaling Strategy for Microservice Applications (Paper 22)

    The assessment of reliability, scalability, and performance in microservice and nanoservice-based systems is the focus of this area. The selected papers examine methods and criteria for evaluating service-oriented architecture effectiveness across a range of deployment situations. Namasudra et al., for example, investigate how blockchain technology might be incorporated into healthcare systems, showing how data confidentiality and integrity can be maintained while improving performance indicators.

    Similar to this, Gkonis et al.'s work discusses the state, difficulties, and unresolved problems associated with IoT-edge-cloud systems, providing information on scalability issues and possible fixes. Wen et al. illustrate how service performance can be optimized in response to shifting needs by putting forth an elastic scaling technique for microservices. When creating effective microservice and nanoservice designs, this category as a whole emphasizes how crucial it is to assess service performance and scalability.


  5. Group 5: Security and Confidentiality in Architectures of Microservices and Nanoservices

    • Dhasarathan et al.: User Privacy Prevention Model Using Supervised Federated Learning-Based Blockchain Approach for Internet of Medical Things (Paper 13)
    • Chen et al.: Research on Internet Security Situation Awareness Prediction Technology Based on Improved RBF Neural Network Algorithm (Paper 14)
    • Xu et al.: Microservice Security Agent Based on API Gateway in Edge Computing (Paper 20)

    In sensitive applications like healthcare, this category focuses on resolving the security and privacy issues that arise with microservice and nanoservice-based architectures. Data integrity and user privacy are protected by the security frameworks and privacy models that are thoroughly examined in the chosen articles. Using cutting-edge methods that reduce privacy concerns in service-oriented architectures, Dhasarathan et al. suggest a federated learning-based strategy to improve privacy protection in IoT systems.

    Furthermore, Chen et al. explore the application of neural network algorithms for situation awareness in internet security, providing a fresh method for recognizing and reacting to security risks instantly. A microservice security agent that uses API gateways to improve security in edge computing environments is covered in the work by Xu et al. When taken as a whole, these studies highlight how important it is to incorporate strong security features into microservice and nanoservice designs in order to safeguard user data and guarantee dependable service delivery.


  6. Group 6: IoT and Edge Computing Applications in Healthcare

    • Abdullayeva: Internet of Things-Based Healthcare System on Patient Demographic Data in Health 4.0 (Paper 12)
    • Sánchez-Gallegos et al.: On the Continuous Processing of Health Data in Edge-Fog-Cloud Computing by Using Micro/Nanoservice Composition (Paper 16)
    • Harjula et al.: Decentralized IoT Edge Nanoservice Architecture for Local Service Provisioning (Paper 17)

    In the healthcare industry, where effective service delivery can have a substantial impact on patient outcomes, this category focuses on the use of microservices and nanoservices. The chosen articles examine how healthcare systems that make use of microservice and nanoservice architectures are designed, implemented, and assessed. In order to show how microservices might improve the usability and accessibility of healthcare services, Abdullayeva's work looks at an Internet of Things-based healthcare system that is centered on patient demographic data.

    In their study of continuous health data processing spanning edge-fog-cloud contexts, Sánchez-Gallegos et al. stress the significance of real-time data management for better patient care. Furthermore, demonstrating how edge computing can enhance service delivery, Harjula et al. suggest a decentralized nanoservice architecture for local service providing in healthcare settings. When taken as a whole, these studies demonstrate how microservices and nanoservices are revolutionizing healthcare applications by guaranteeing prompt and effective access to vital services.


  7. Group 7: Research and Simulations in Theory

    • Kim et al.: Control Theory Approach for Task Offloading in Cloud-Edge Computing
    • Sok et al.: Reinforcement Learning-Based Task Offloading Strategies in IoT

    This category emphasizes research and theoretical work that lays the groundwork for practical applications. The selected studies aim to advance the fundamental understanding of microservice and nanoservice deployments through simulations and control theory models. For instance, Kim et al. take a control theory approach to task offloading in cloud-edge computing, providing important insights into optimizing resource usage.

    Furthermore, Sok et al. propose reinforcement learning-based strategies for task offloading in IoT settings, highlighting how these methods can enhance service efficiency and resource management. Theoretical advancements in this area are essential for supporting the practical applications that are discussed in other categories, thereby linking research with implementation.
